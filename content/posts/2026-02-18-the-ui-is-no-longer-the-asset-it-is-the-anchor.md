For decades, the moat for companies like Salesforce was the UI. If you controlled the screen where the work happened, you controlled the data. You owned the user.

But we are entering the era of the Legacy Database Pivot, and the ground is shifting.

**The Utopian View:** AI will simply bolt onto existing platforms, making Salesforce even more dominant as it automates our current workflows.

**The Doomer View:** Salesforce is a dinosaur that will be replaced overnight by nimble AI-native startups.

**The Middle Lane Reality:** Salesforce isn't going away, but its role is being fundamentally demoted. In the near future, Salesforce will transition from being the "Operating System of Sales" to being just a very reliable database.

**The LLM will be the new UI.**

Employees won’t log in to navigate 50 tabs and complex dashboards. They will work through a seamless AI agent that sits over the data, pulling what it needs and executing actions across multiple systems simultaneously.\
For many software companies, this is a crisis of identity. When you lose the UI, you lose the seat. And when you lose the seat, the traditional SaaS pricing model becomes obsolete.

The takeaway for investors and founders: Stop betting on companies that have an AI feature. Start betting on the infrastructure that allows LLMs to navigate legacy databases with zero friction. The value is migrating from the front end experience to the back end intelligence.
