AI logic is rapidly approaching a marginal cost of zero. As AGI synthesizes workflows and code instantly, the competitive advantage of "how" a company operates is evaporating. We are entering an era where the only remaining value is the integrity of the output and the repository that holds it.

## **The Binary Trap**

The Utopian view suggests that free logic leads to a frictionless world where agents handle every task autonomously. Conversely, the Doomer perspective warns that when the "how" becomes effortless, human expertise becomes obsolete and systems become unmanageable. Both sides miss the structural reality. Logic without a High-Fidelity Repository is just noise. If everyone can generate the same perfect logic, the logic itself is no longer the product.

## **The Middle Lane Pivot**

The shift moves us from Workflow-based SaaS to Resolution as a Service (RaaS). In the old model, you paid for the tools to perform logic. In the RaaS model, you pay for the verified outcome. Software is no longer a set of buttons. It is a High-Fidelity Repository that ensures the "Ghost in the Machine" actually delivered what it promised. The value is not in the agentic mirage of doing work, but in the infrastructure that proves the work is correct.

## **The Pricing Audit**

Seat-based pricing is the primary casualty of free logic. If an agent does the work of ten people, charging per human head is a race to the bottom. We apply the 1-to-4 Rule: for every dollar saved in logic production, four dollars of value must be captured in outcome integrity. Companies must transition to outcome-based SaaS pricing. You are no longer selling the labor. You are selling the resolution.

## **The Pragmatic Solution**

Stop over-investing in the "intelligence" of your agents and start investing in the "integrity" of your data backend. Build systems that act as the final arbiter of truth. Ensure your RaaS architecture can audit agentic outputs in real time. The winners of 2026 will not be those with the smartest bots, but those with the most reliable repositories.
